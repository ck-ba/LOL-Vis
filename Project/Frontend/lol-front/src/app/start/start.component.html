League of Legends Visualizations

Overview

This tool visualizes the positions of players' champions of League of Legends Matches Champion Positions are fetched via a backend connecting to the official API provided by Riot Games. Once the data is fetched, it will usually be displayed via groups of 3 heatmaps. The left map will show the positions of team blue, the middle of team red, and the right of both teams mixed.

The positions can and should be filtered by game time, as a heat map of positions of the whole game in one picture will not have much meaning. Filtering by game time is done via a slider that progressively lets you choose the game-time intervals to be displayed. There are different interval lengths available to view game positions of 1, 2, 5, 10 or 15 minute intervals. Be careful if you choose smaller time intervals (1,2 or 5 minutes) when you look at position data of a small number of matches, which can happen depending on your query! You might only get a random number of dots instead of coherent movement data, especially at later game times.

There are 3 different options to create these Heatmaps


You can search the match history of one specific player, either looking at all their matches or filtering further. The position data of all team members will be displayed, not just of the one player.

You can filter by the champion the player chose (champion name), the team they played in (red/blue), and if you chose a team, you can also filter by whether the player's team won or lost.


You can search the match history of one specific player, either looking at all their matches, matches where they played one specific champion or filtering further. The position data of only their own champion's positions will be displayed.

You can enter a champion the player chose (champion name), and can additionally filter by the team they played in (red/blue).

If you don't filter by champions, the map will likely not be very useful depending on how many matches the player played and whether they play different lanes in general.

If you filter by team, you will not get 3 maps as usual, but instead two maps that show how the chosen champion in the chosen team moved in matches that were won (left map) or lost (right map)


This option intends to give you a more general picture of how players position themselves in a Lol Match. You do not search one player's match history, but instead get matches of several different players' matches. The position data of all players' positions will be displayed. Again, filter options are available.

You can either look at matches in general or choose both a team (red/blue) and that team's outcome (win/lose).

Additionally, you can choose a sample size of how many random matches that match the query will be taken for creating the visualizations. If the sample size too small, the visualizations might not portray a balanced picture of average matches. If the sample size is larger than the amount of matches matches the query, all found matches will instead be fetched.
